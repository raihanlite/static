How to Use the CGPA Calculator

Step 1 — Calculate Your Semester GPA

Under the Semester Calculator, enter each subject name, its credit hours, and your grade. Click Add Subject after each one. The Semester GPA updates automatically as you add subjects.

Step 2 — Save Your Semester Records

Once your current semester is complete, move to the Overall History panel. Select the semester number, enter the GPA and total credits you just calculated, and click Save Semester. Repeat for each past semester. The Cumulative CGPA at the top updates instantly.

Step 3 — Plan Your Target CGPA

In the Target CGPA Planner, enter your desired CGPA and the number of credit hours you have left. Click Analyze Target CGPA to see the average GPA you need in future courses to reach your goal.

CGPA Formula Explained

All Bangladesh universities under the University Grants Commission (UGC) calculate CGPA using the weighted average method:

CGPA = Σ (Credit Hours × Grade Point) ÷ Σ (Credit Hours)

For example: if you take Mathematics (3 credits, A+ = 4.00) and Physics (3 credits, A = 3.75), your GPA is (3×4.00 + 3×3.75) ÷ 6 = 23.25 ÷ 6 = 3.875.

Why Credit Hours Matter

A 3-credit course contributes three times more to your CGPA than a 1-credit course. This is why doing well in higher-credit subjects (like core engineering or business courses) has a bigger impact than optional 1-credit courses.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is CGPA calculated in Bangladesh universities?

CGPA = Σ(Credit Hours × Grade Point) ÷ Σ(Credit Hours). All UGC-affiliated universities follow a 4.0 scale where A+ = 4.00, A = 3.75, A− = 3.50, and so on. The formula weights each course by its credit hours so higher-credit subjects have more impact.

What CGPA is considered good in Bangladesh?

A CGPA of 3.50 or above is considered excellent. 3.00–3.49 is good, 2.50–2.99 is average, and below 2.50 may affect scholarship eligibility. Many graduate programs and employers look for a minimum of 3.00.

Can I calculate CGPA for all 8 semesters?

Yes. Use Step 1 to calculate each semester's GPA, then add each one to the Overall History in Step 2. The calculator combines all saved records into your true cumulative CGPA automatically.

Does BUET use the same grading scale as other universities?

BUET uses a slightly different scale — for instance, A = 4.00 (not 3.75 as in the UGC standard). The university preset dropdown adjusts the grade points accordingly for BUET and other institutions that differ from UGC standard.

Is my data saved or sent anywhere?

No. All calculations run entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server and no account is required. Your data disappears when you close or refresh the page.
