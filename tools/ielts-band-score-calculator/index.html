How IELTS Overall Band Score is Calculated

Your overall IELTS band score is the simple average of the four section bands — Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking — rounded to the nearest whole or half band using a specific rule.

The Official IELTS Rounding Rule (.25 / .75)

Once you add all four section scores and divide by 4, the decimal part determines how the result is rounded:

• Average ending in .00 or .50 → reported as-is (e.g., 6.50 → 6.5)

• Average ending in .25 → rounded up to next .5 (e.g., 6.25 → 6.5)

• Average ending in .75 → rounded up to next whole number (e.g., 7.75 → 8.0)

For example: Listening 7.0 + Reading 6.5 + Writing 6.0 + Speaking 6.5 = 26.0 ÷ 4 = 6.50 — reported as 6.5.

Another example: L 7.0 + R 7.0 + W 6.5 + S 6.0 = 26.5 ÷ 4 = 6.625 — rounds down to 6.5.

Academic vs. General Training: Reading Differences

Both IELTS modules contain 40 Reading questions, but the conversion from raw scores to band scores differs significantly. General Training Reading texts are generally longer and more varied, so the band boundaries are set higher to compensate.

Typical IELTS Score Requirements

UK Universities

Most undergraduate programs require 6.0–6.5 overall, with no band below 5.5. Postgraduate programs typically require 6.5–7.0.

Australian Migration

General Skilled Migration (subclass 189/190) requires 6.0 overall with no band below 6.0 for 10 points; 7.0+ for higher points allocations.

Canadian Immigration

Express Entry (Federal Skilled Worker) requires minimum CLB 7, roughly equivalent to IELTS 6.0 in each band for listening and reading, 6.0 for writing and speaking.

US Universities

Most US universities accept IELTS starting at 6.5 overall for undergraduate and 7.0 for graduate admissions. Top-tier schools may require 7.5+.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the IELTS overall band score calculated?

The overall band is the average of Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. That average is rounded to the nearest whole or half band using the .25/.75 rule: .25 rounds up to the next .5, and .75 rounds up to the next whole number.

Can I get a band like 6.25 or 7.25?

No. IELTS only reports whole bands (7.0) or half bands (7.5). A calculated average of 7.25 rounds up to 7.5 by the official rule, so you will never see a score like 7.25 on your Test Report Form.

Is there negative marking in IELTS?

No. Only correct answers are counted. There is no penalty for wrong answers, so you should always attempt every question and never leave a blank.

Why are Academic and General Training Reading scores different?

The two modules test different skills. Academic Reading uses more complex texts, so fewer correct answers are needed to achieve a given band. General Training Reading requires more correct answers for the same band, as its texts are generally more accessible.

What IELTS score do I need for a UK visa or university?

Requirements vary. Most UK universities require 6.0–7.0 overall. The UK Skilled Worker visa uses a minimum of Band 4.0 on its own scale, but Tier 1/2 visas and university requirements are significantly higher. Always check with the specific institution or immigration authority.

Can I retake just one section of IELTS?

Yes, with IELTS One Skill Retake (introduced in 2023), you can retake a single section — Listening, Reading, Writing, or Speaking — within 60 days of your original test, rather than sitting the entire exam again.
