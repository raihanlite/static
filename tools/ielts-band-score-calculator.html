The Ultimate Guide to IELTS Band Scores

Understanding how your IELTS scores are calculated is the first step toward achieving your target band. Whether you are taking the IELTS Academic for university or General Training for migration, the scoring system follows a strict standardized process.

What is IELTS Band Score?

The IELTS (International English Language Testing System) uses a 9-band scale to measure English language proficiency. Band 9 represents an expert level of English, while Band 1 indicates minimal ability. Scores are reported in whole and half bands (e.g., 6.5, 7.0), allowing for precise assessment of your language skills.

How the Overall Band Score is Calculated

Your overall result is the average of the four components: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. However, IELTS uses a unique rounding system to ensure fairness.

The .25 and .75 Rule:
  • If your average ends in .25 , it is rounded UP to the next half band (e.g., 6.25 becomes 6.5).
  • If your average ends in .75 , it is rounded UP to the next whole band (e.g., 7.75 becomes 8.0).
  • If the average is .125 or .625 , it is rounded DOWN to the nearest half/whole band.

Academic vs. General Training Reading

A common point of confusion is the Reading section. While the Listening scores are identical across both modules, the Reading scales differ:

Band Score Academic (Raw) General (Raw)
8.0 35-36 37-38
7.0 30-32 34-35
6.0 23-26 30-31

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is there negative marking in IELTS?

A: No. You are not penalized for incorrect answers. It is always better to guess than to leave a blank space.

Q: Can I get a 7.25?

A: No. IELTS only awards whole (7.0) or half (7.5) bands. A calculated average of 7.25 will be officially reported as a 7.5.

Q: Who accepts IELTS scores?

A: Over 11,000 organizations worldwide, including universities in the UK, Australia, Canada, and the USA, as well as immigration departments.
