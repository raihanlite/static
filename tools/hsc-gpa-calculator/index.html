How to Use the HSC GPA Calculator

Step 1 — Select Your Group

Click the group that matches your HSC programme:

  • Science — Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Math, ICT, Bangla, English
  • Business Studies — Accounting, Business Org. & Management, Finance, Banking & Insurance, ICT, Bangla, English
  • Humanities — Civics, History, Geography & Environment, ICT, Bangla, English

The subject list updates automatically when you switch groups.

Step 2 — Enter Marks or Select a Grade

For each subject you can enter your result in two ways: type the raw marks in the number box, or choose a grade from the dropdown. You only need to fill in one — entering marks auto-selects the corresponding grade.

Step 3 — Add an Optional Subject (if any)

If you sat a 4th optional subject, select it from the Optional Subject dropdown, then enter your marks or grade. Leave it blank if you did not take one.

Step 4 — Click Calculate GPA

Hit the Calculate GPA button. The calculator instantly shows your final GPA (out of 5.00) and your approximate total marks.

Step 5 — Save Your Result

Use the Save Result as Image button to download a PNG of your result — perfect for sharing on social media or keeping a record.

How HSC GPA is Calculated

The HSC GPA formula used by Bangladesh education boards is straightforward:

  1. Convert each subject's marks to a grade point using the grading scale.
  2. Add all grade points for the main subjects and divide by the number of subjects.
  3. If you have an optional subject with grade point above 2.00, add the bonus: (GP − 2) ÷ 2.
  4. The final result is capped at 5.00.

For example: if your 6 main subjects have grade points 5, 5, 4, 4, 4, 3.5 → total = 25.5 ÷ 6 = 4.25 GPA.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum GPA in HSC?

The maximum possible GPA in HSC Bangladesh is 5.00, achieved by scoring A+ (80–100 marks) in all subjects.

Does the optional subject increase GPA?

Yes — but only if your optional subject grade point is above 2.00 (C grade). The bonus added to your GPA is calculated as (grade point − 2) ÷ 2. For example, an A+ (5.00) in optional adds 1.50 bonus points.

Why is ICT out of 100 and not 200?

ICT (Information and Communication Technology) carries a total of 100 marks in the HSC curriculum — 25 marks for practical and 75 for theory — while most other subjects carry 200 marks in total.

Can I calculate GPA without knowing my exact marks?

Yes. Instead of entering marks, simply select the grade (A+, A, A−, B, C, D, or F) from the grade dropdown for each subject. The calculator will use the standard grade point value for that letter grade.

Is this calculator valid for all Bangladesh education boards?

Yes. The grading system and GPA calculation method used here applies to all boards under the Bangladesh Board of Intermediate and Secondary Education (BISE), including Dhaka, Rajshahi, Comilla, Chittagong, Sylhet, Barisal, Dinajpur, Mymensingh, and the Madrasah board.
