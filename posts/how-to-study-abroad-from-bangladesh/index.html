How to Study Abroad from Bangladesh — Complete Guide 2026

Everything a Bangladeshi student needs to know: choosing a country, IELTS requirements, scholarships, the visa process, and what to expect when you arrive.

📅 Updated: March 2026
⏱ 15 min read
✍️ Study Koro

Every year, tens of thousands of Bangladeshi students leave for universities in the UK, Australia, Canada, the United States, Germany, Malaysia, and beyond. Studying abroad opens doors to world-class education, international work experience, and often a pathway to residency in a new country. But the process — from choosing a university to landing at the airport with a student visa in hand — can feel overwhelming if you don't know where to start.

This guide walks you through every stage of the journey, step by step, with specific information relevant to students from Bangladesh.

Why Study Abroad from Bangladesh?

Bangladesh has made enormous strides in its domestic university sector, but studying at a recognized international institution still carries significant advantages for Bangladeshi students:

  • Global degree recognition — Degrees from UK, Australian, Canadian, and American universities are accepted by employers worldwide without question.
  • Post-study work rights — The UK's Graduate Route visa (2 years), Australia's Temporary Graduate visa (2–4 years), and Canada's Post-Graduation Work Permit (up to 3 years) let you gain international work experience after graduating.
  • Pathways to residency — Canada and Australia in particular have structured immigration systems where studying locally can significantly strengthen a permanent residency application.
  • Research and facilities — Access to research labs, libraries, and academic networks that are simply unavailable in Bangladesh for most fields.
  • Personal growth — Language fluency, cultural adaptability, and independence are qualities that employers across every industry increasingly value.

Step-by-Step: How to Study Abroad from Bangladesh

1

Decide What You Want to Study and Why

Before choosing a country or university, be clear on your field, level (undergraduate or postgraduate), and career goal. Different countries have different strengths — engineering in Germany, business in the UK, healthcare in Australia, technology in Canada. Your choice of field should drive your choice of destination.

2

Check Your Academic Eligibility

Most undergraduate programs abroad require your SSC and HSC results. A GPA of 4.00+ in HSC (out of 5.00) is generally competitive. For postgraduate programs, your bachelor's degree transcript is the key document — a CGPA of 3.00+ (out of 4.00) from a recognized Bangladesh university is typically the minimum. Use our HSC GPA Calculator and CGPA Calculator to verify your scores.

3

Take IELTS (or an Accepted Alternative)

For English-medium programs in the UK, Australia, Canada, and USA, an English language test is mandatory. IELTS Academic is the most widely accepted. Aim for at least 6.5 overall for most universities. See the IELTS requirements section below for country-by-country details.

4

Research Universities and Programs

Use official university websites, QS World University Rankings, and the Times Higher Education rankings. Apply to a range of universities — 2–3 reach schools, 3–4 target schools, and 2 safe options. Pay attention to entry requirements, tuition fees, and scholarship availability for international students.

5

Apply for Scholarships Early

Scholarships have earlier deadlines than university applications — sometimes by 3–6 months. Research government scholarships (Chevening, Australia Awards, Commonwealth) and university merit scholarships simultaneously with your applications.

6

Submit University Applications

Prepare your Statement of Purpose (SOP), academic transcripts, recommendation letters (usually 2–3), CV, and proof of English proficiency. Deadlines vary — UK universities via UCAS typically close in January for September intake; Australian and Canadian universities often have rolling admissions.

7

Apply for Your Student Visa

Once you have a Conditional or Unconditional Offer and have accepted it, you can apply for a student visa. You will need proof of funds (typically covering 1 year of tuition + living costs), your offer letter, IELTS results, and passport photos. Apply at least 3 months before your course start date.

8

Prepare to Depart

Arrange student accommodation (university halls or private), open a student bank account in your destination country (many banks let you do this online before arriving), sort travel and health insurance, and attend any pre-departure orientation offered by your university or the Bangladesh embassy.

Choosing the Right Country

United Kingdom

The UK remains one of the most popular destinations for Bangladeshi students. Undergraduate degrees take 3 years (saving one year vs Bangladesh or the US), and the Graduate Route visa allows 2 years of post-study work after graduation (3 years for PhD graduates). Tuition fees range from £10,000 to £38,000 per year. Living costs in London are significantly higher than other cities — consider universities in Manchester, Birmingham, Leeds, or Sheffield for better value.

Australia

Australia offers a high quality of life and strong student work rights — you can work up to 48 hours per fortnight while studying. The Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) allows 2–4 years of work rights after graduation, depending on the qualification and state where you study. Tuition fees range from AUD 20,000 to AUD 45,000 per year. Sydney and Melbourne are expensive; consider Brisbane, Adelaide, or Perth.

Canada

Canada has one of the clearest pathways from student to permanent resident. The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) allows up to 3 years of work after completing a degree of 2+ years. Points earned from a Canadian degree and work experience can be used in the Express Entry immigration system. Tuition varies widely — CAD 15,000 to CAD 35,000 per year for international students.

Germany

Public universities in Germany charge little to no tuition fees for international students — you typically pay only a semester fee of €150–400. The catch is that most programs at the bachelor's level are taught in German. However, Germany has a rapidly growing number of English-taught master's programs, particularly in engineering, computer science, and business. Living costs are moderate at approximately €800–1,100 per month.

Malaysia

Malaysia is an increasingly popular and affordable alternative for Bangladeshi students. Tuition fees are significantly lower (MYR 15,000–60,000 per year), the cost of living is manageable, and many universities offer programs taught in English. It is also geographically and culturally closer to Bangladesh, making it easier for students who haven't studied or lived far from home before.

Scholarships for Bangladeshi Students

Funding your education abroad is the biggest challenge most Bangladeshi families face. The good news: there are dozens of scholarships specifically open to Bangladeshi students.

Government-Funded Scholarships

  • Chevening Scholarship (UK) — Fully funded by the UK government for outstanding future leaders. Covers tuition, living allowance, flights, and visa. For postgraduate study only. Deadline is typically November each year.
  • Australia Awards Scholarship — Fully funded by the Australian government for students from developing countries. Covers tuition, travel, Overseas Student Health Cover, and a living allowance. Very competitive — apply 12–18 months in advance.
  • Commonwealth Scholarship — For students from Commonwealth countries including Bangladesh. Covers full study costs for master's and PhD programs at UK universities.
  • DAAD Scholarship (Germany) — The German Academic Exchange Service offers numerous scholarships for Bangladeshi students for study and research in Germany. Many cover tuition, health insurance, and a monthly stipend of around €850–1,200.
  • Erasmus+ Scholarship (Europe) — Supports study or traineeships at European universities. Bangladesh students at eligible partner universities can apply.
  • China Government Scholarship (CSC) — Fully funded scholarships for undergraduate, master's, and PhD study at Chinese universities. No tuition fees, free accommodation, health insurance, and a monthly stipend.

University Merit Scholarships

Most universities in the UK, Australia, and Canada offer merit-based scholarships or fee reductions for high-achieving international students. These range from 10% to 100% of tuition. Check each university's international scholarships page — deadlines are usually the same as or shortly after the application deadline.

💡 Tip

Apply for scholarships and universities simultaneously. Many scholarship applications require a university offer letter, so begin both processes at the same time rather than waiting for admission before starting your scholarship search.

Documents You Will Need

  • Valid passport — At least 18 months validity beyond your course end date
  • SSC and HSC transcripts and certificates — Attested by the Bangladesh education board
  • Bachelor's degree transcript and provisional certificate (for postgraduate applicants)
  • IELTS Academic result — Original TRF (Test Report Form), usually valid for 2 years
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) — 500–1,000 words explaining why you want to study this program
  • 2–3 Reference Letters — From academic supervisors or professors who know your work
  • CV/Resume — Academic and extracurricular achievements
  • Bank statement — Showing sufficient funds (usually 12 months of tuition + living costs)
  • Passport-size photographs — Requirements vary by country
  • Health insurance / OSHC — Required before visa issuance in Australia

Frequently Asked Questions

What IELTS score do I need to study abroad from Bangladesh?

Most programs in the UK, Australia, and Canada require 6.0–6.5 overall for undergraduate and 6.5–7.0 for postgraduate. No individual band should fall below 5.5 in most cases. Use our IELTS calculator to check your estimated score.

How much money do I need to show in my bank for a student visa?

Requirements vary by country. For a UK Student visa, you typically need to show funds covering 9 months of tuition + £1,334/month living costs (outside London) or £1,023/month (in London). For Australia, you need to show AUD 21,041 per year for living expenses, plus your full tuition. For Canada, you need CAD 10,000 per year above tuition. These figures are updated periodically — check the official embassy website before applying.

Can I study abroad from Bangladesh without IELTS?

Some universities accept TOEFL, PTE Academic, or Duolingo English Test as alternatives. Germany, Japan, and China offer programs in languages other than English. A few UK universities also waive IELTS if your previous education was conducted entirely in English — check individual institution policies.

Which country is easiest for Bangladeshi students to get a student visa?

Malaysia and Germany have relatively straightforward student visa processes for Bangladeshi nationals. The UK and Australia are more rigorous but manageable with proper documentation. Canada has become stricter in recent years; ensure your financial documents are clear and your study permit application is complete.

Can I work while studying abroad?

Yes, in most countries. The UK allows 20 hours/week during term time. Australia allows 48 hours per fortnight. Canada allows 20 hours/week off-campus. Germany allows 120 full working days per year. Always check the specific conditions of your visa — working beyond permitted hours can jeopardize your visa status.
